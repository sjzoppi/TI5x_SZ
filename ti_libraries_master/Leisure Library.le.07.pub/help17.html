Nim is a game where two players take turns removing chips from a common pile. The object of the game is to force the other player to take the last chip.

In this version of Nim you may play with up to 94 chips. The calculator acts in the capacity of one of the players and you are the other. Each player must remove either 1, 2, or 3 chips per turn.

A special feature of this program indicated by the title is that it has the ability to "learn". So watch out! The next time around the calculator will play a better game.

  • Initialize program :  SBR   CLR . 0 is displayed.
  • Enter the number (max 94) of chips you wish to use and press  R/S .
  • If you which to make the first move, press  A . The number of chips in the pile is displayed.
  • If you want the calculator to make the first move, press  B . The calculator then makes its move and displays the number of chips remaining in the pule.
  • Enter the number of chips you wish to take and press  R/S . The calculator then makes its move and returns the number of chips remaining after its move. Repeat this step until the display flashes. If 0 is flashed, you win. If π is flashed, the calculator wins.
  • For a new game, press CE  E . The number of chips used in the last game is displayed.
