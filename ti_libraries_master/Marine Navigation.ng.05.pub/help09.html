NG-O9 — Course Made Good from Three Bearings

This program will calculate the course made good (CMG) on the basis of three separate bearings on a single object.  Data that must be supplied includes the magnetic variation (V) and three compass bearings in conjunction with their corresponding times of observation.

The procedure outlined below is used to determine the course made good.

First,
N =
 ( t3 - t2) sin (Bc1 - Bc2)
( t2 - t1) sin (Bc2 - Bc3)

is evaluated.

The, ( x ,  y ) is converted to the polar representation ( r , θ ) where:
  • x = cos( Bc1 ) - N cos( Bc3 )
  • y = sin( Bc1 ) - N sin( Bc3 )
Now,
  • CMG = θ - V - De   (0º ≤ CMG < 360º).
The additional variables used in the above equations are
t1,2,3
 =
 the times of the observations

and
Bc1,2,3
 =
 the compass bearings of the object at the observation times.

REMARKS:

  1. The vessel speed and course through the water must remain constant between the first and third observations for correct results.
  2. The magnetic deviation may be diregarded.
