NG-10 — Dead Reckoning

As is indicated by the title, this program determines the dead reckoning position after being given the starting position ( LS , λS ) and time ( tS ), the true course (CMG) and speed (SMG) made good, and the time of dead reckoning ( tDR ).

LDR = ( Δt × SMG × cos(CMG) ) / 60 + LS.

In the above equations

  • LS,DR = the latitudes of the starting and dead reckoning positions (Northern latitudes are considered positive and southern latitudes are given negative values.), and
  • λS,DR = the longitudes of the starting and dead reckoning positions. (Western latitudes are assigned positive values and eastern latitudes are considered to be negative.)

REMARKS:

  1. This routine cannot follow a meridian over a pole and loses accuracy when within 0.5° of a pole.
  2. Speed and course made good must remain constant for individual legs.
